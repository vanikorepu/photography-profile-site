About

Adam Quiros captures the quiet dignity of America's roadside relics—vintage motel signs, weathered restaurant facades, and mid-century architecture slowly surrendering to time.

His lens finds poetry in peeling paint and beauty in rust. Each photograph is a small act of preservation, documenting the neon dreams and desert modernism of a vanishing American landscape.

From Route 66 diners to forgotten motor lodges, Adam's work celebrates the typography, design, and stories embedded in these everyday monuments before they disappear entirely.
